WHAT IS TRIPLE NEGATIVE? 

TRIPLE NEGATIVE breast cancer, which is about 20% of all cases, lacks both the hormone receptor as well as the HER-2 receptor on the cancer cell.  That can make it one of the more difficult breast cancers to treat.  Triple negative also tends to affect younger women and ethnic minorities, as well as those with the BRCA mutation. 

     Triple negative breast cancer is harder to cure, and has a significant recurrence rate. Because of this, the development of new drugs and treatments to help improve survival is crucial.  Being in a clinical trial allows you access to those drugs that otherwise are not yet approved.  commercially available. 


 

YOUR MAIN BENEFITS

   You will be receiving Standard of Care for the cancer, in addition to the new drug treatments which are being tested. You will be followed by a research team and be treated by a Board Certified Cancer Specialist, or Oncologist. These drugs are presently NOT approved to treat triple negative breast cancer. That is the purpose of the research trial...to see if they can help. These drugs are provided to you without any additional cost.
